# Go-to-Market Strategy

This document outlines DoctorLink's market entry and expansion strategy.

---

## Phase 1: Launch (Months 1-6)

### Target Market: South Africa

**Rationale:**
- Best infrastructure (internet, mobile, payments)
- Clear regulatory framework (HPCSA)
- Largest economy in Africa
- English-speaking
- Established private healthcare market

### Launch Strategy

#### Doctor Acquisition (Month 1-3)
| Channel | Target | Cost |
|---------|-------|------|
| Medical associations | 50 doctors | Low |
| Social media targeting | 30 doctors | Medium |
| Referral program | 20 doctors | Low |
| Medical schools (alumni) | 10 doctors | Low |
| **Total** | **110 doctors** | |

#### Patient Acquisition (Month 4-6)
| Channel | Target | Cost |
|---------|-------|------|
| Social media (Facebook, Instagram) | 2,000 users | High |
| Google search ads | 3,000 users | High |
| Partner clinics (referrals) | 1,000 users | Low |
| Influencer partnerships | 500 users | Medium |
| Employer wellness programs | 2,000 users | Medium |
| **Total** | **8,500 users** | |

#### Launch Tactics
1. **Freemium model**: First consultation free
2. **Doctor referral**: Earn credits for referrals
3. **Employer pilots**: Partner with 5-10 companies
4. **Clinic partnerships**: Integration with existing clinics

### Metrics Target (Phase 1)
- Doctors: 100+ active
- Patients: 5,000+ registered
- Consultations: 1,000+
- Satisfaction: 4.5+ stars

## Sales Strategy

### B2C Model
- **Self-service**: App/website booking
- **Assisted**: Phone support for elderly/non-tech
- **Concierge**: Premium tier for high-value patients

### B2B Model
- **Sales team**: 2-3 people dedicated
- **Target**: Companies with 100+ employees
- **Process**: Pilot → Contract → Scale
- **Pricing**: Per-employee-per-month (PEPM)

### Enterprise Pricing (B2B)
| Tier | Employees | Price/employee/month |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Basic | 100-500 | $3-5 |
| Standard | 500-2000 | $5-8 |
| Enterprise | 2000+ | $8-12 |

## Operational Strategy

### Doctor Operations
1. **Onboarding**: 48-hour verification, training
2. **Quality**: Rating system, minimum standards
3. **Support**: 24/7 doctor support line
4. **Incentives**: Bonuses for high ratings, volume
5. **Retention**: Revenue share, recognition, tools

### Patient Operations
1. **Onboarding**: Simple signup, verification
2. **Support**: In-app chat, phone support
3. **Experience**: Post-consultation follow-up
4. **Retention**: Credits, loyalty program
5. **Feedback**: Ratings, surveys

### Technology Operations
- **Infrastructure**: AWS (primary), backup providers
- **Video**: WebRTC, low-bandwidth mode
- **Security**: HIPAA-level, encryption
- **Reliability**: 99.9% uptime SLA

## Competitive Response Strategy

### If Teladoc/Amwell Enter Africa
- Accelerate doctor acquisition
- Highlight local expertise
- Emphasize gig model differentiation
- Consider acquisition discussions

### If Local Competitors Emerge
- Strengthen doctor relationships (revenue share)
- Expand service offerings
- Build brand loyalty
- Maintain pricing advantage

### Sustaining Competitive Advantage
1. **Doctor network**: First-mover network effects
2. **Brand**: Trust and recognition
3. **Data**: Learning from consultations
4. **Partnerships**: Institutional relationships
5. **Technology**: Continuous innovation
