## 26. Teladoc Health - Global Telehealth Giant

**Country**: USA  
**Founded**: 2002  
**Valuation**: $20B (at peak), now ~$3B  
**Revenue**: $2B+ annually

### Business Model
- **Subscription Model**: B2B employers and health plans
- **Per Visit Fees**: Direct consumer
- **Specialty Services**: Chronic condition management, mental health
- **Acquisition Strategy**: 20+ acquisitions including Livongo, InTouch, Best Doctors

### Key Metrics
- **50M+** virtual visits completed
- **20+ years** of experience
- **3,000+** employees
- **175+ countries** served

### Success Factors
1. **B2B Focus**: Stable revenue from employers/insurers
2. **Acquisition Strategy**: Roll-up strategy for expansion
3. **Full Range**: General medicine to specialty care
4. **Scale**: Global reach enables network effects

### Lessons for DoctorLink
- B2B provides revenue stability
- Acquisitions can accelerate growth
- Full-stack offering is valuable
- Global scale creates moats

## Summary: Global Patterns

| Pattern | Frequency | Examples |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| B2B/B2B2C | 70% | Teladoc, Amwell, K Health |
| Subscription | 65% | Babylon, Hims, Roman |
| Insurance Integration | 40% | Amwell, Ping An, LiveHealth |
| AI/Data | 35% | K Health, Babylon, Ping An |
| Acquisitions | 30% | Teladoc, MDLive, Hims |
| Geographic Focus | 50% | Most companies |
| Full Stack | 45% | Teladoc, Babylon, Ro |

### Key Lessons for DoctorLink

1. **B2B First**: Most successful companies go B2B for stable revenue
2. **Subscription Drives LTV**: Recurring revenue is key to valuation
3. **Insurance Integration**: Where possible, enables volume
4. **AI is Augmentation**: Not replacement - enhances doctors
5. **Acquisition Strategy**: Roll-up can accelerate growth
6. **Local Focus**: Deep in one market > thin everywhere
7. **Compliance is Barrier**: Regulatory is significant hurdle
